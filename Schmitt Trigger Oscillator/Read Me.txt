# Schmitt Trigger Oscillator

This project implements a CMOS Schmitt trigger oscillator designed to generate a 20 kHz square wave from a 250 kHz square-wave input. The design uses a Schmitt trigger core, a switched-capacitor feedback network in place of a large resistor, and an inverter chain to drive capacitive loads.

## Overview

The oscillator uses hysteresis in an inverting Schmitt trigger with RC-style feedback to generate a square-wave output. Frequency and duty cycle are set by the trigger thresholds and the effective time constant of the feedback network.

To avoid implementing a very large on-chip resistor in a 65 nm process, the feedback resistance is realized with a switched-capacitor circuit. A parallel pMOS branch was added to the original nMOS pass network to correct rise/fall imbalance and produce a more balanced waveform at the Schmitt trigger input.

## Design

- Schmitt trigger topology with feedback transistors controlling threshold levels.
- Feedback transistor width ratios set to 1 for pMOS and 0.6 for nMOS.
- Measured hysteresis thresholds: 660 mV low threshold and 790 mV high threshold.
- Switched-capacitor feedback network with a 100 fF switch capacitor.
- Output inverter chain sized to support loads from 10 fF to 10 pF.
- Initial inverter-chain estimate: 10 stages with a multiplier of 4 for the full load target.

## Specifications

- Input signal: 250 kHz square wave.
- Target output: 20 kHz square wave.
- Target duty cycle: 50% ± 1%.
- Startup time target: less than 10 µs.
- Rise/fall time target: less than 10 ns.
- Schmitt trigger low threshold target: 0.6 V ± 0.1 V.
- Schmitt trigger high threshold target: 0.8 V ± 0.1 V.
- Output low/high target: below 100 mV and above 1.1 V.
- DC current draw target at logic low/high input: less than 5 µA.

## Results

- Simulated output frequency: 19.25 kHz.
- Simulated duty cycle: 49.8%.
- Startup time: less than 0.2 µs.
- Rise/fall times: 14 ps and 12 ps.
- Simulated Schmitt trigger thresholds: 0.67 V low and 0.78 V high.
- Output levels: 130 nV low and 1.2 V high.
- DC current draw at input logic levels: less than 732 nA.
- Peak current draw during DC sweep: 7.48 fA at 370 mV.

## Layout

The physical layout was simplified from the original schematic to reduce implementation complexity. The inverter chain was reduced from 10 stages to 2 in layout, and capacitors were replaced with CRTMOM equivalents close to the schematic values.

DRC completed with only 4 latch-up errors reported, which were treated as negligible for this process. LVS did not pass because of an unresolved connection, port, or net mismatch, so the layout was not fully fabrication-ready in its current state.
